About — Nine Men's Morris

Game Rules

A classic strategy board game known since the Roman Empire.

How to play:

  1. Placing phase: Players take turns placing pieces on empty intersections.
  2. Moving phase: When all 9 pieces are placed, players move pieces along lines to adjacent spots.
  3. Flying phase: When you have only 3 pieces left, you can 'fly' them to any empty spot on the board.

Mills: Form a row of 3 pieces (a Mill) to remove an opponent's piece.

Winning: Reduce your opponent to 2 pieces or block all their legal moves.

AI built with Minimax algorithm & Alpha-Beta pruning.

Play the Classic Nine Men's Morris Game Online

Welcome to our unique browser-based version of one of the oldest and most classic board games in the world — Nine Men's Morris (also known historically as Merels or Młynek). This free-to-play web application perfectly combines the ancient strategical depth of the board game with a magnificent, nostalgic graphical user interface inspired by the classic Windows 98 operating system.

What is Nine Men's Morris?

Nine Men's Morris is an abstract strategy game whose origins trace back to the Roman Empire and Ancient Egypt. While the rules are remarkably easy to pick up, mastering the game requires deep tactical foresight and intense logical planning, similar to standard chess or checkers. Our digital iteration is powered by an extremely robust Minimax algorithm with Alpha-Beta pruning, providing a competitive, intelligent AI capable of challenging even the most experienced players across 3 distinct difficulty levels.

The Main Phases of the Game:

  • Placing Phase: Both players start the game with nine pieces (or "men"). Players take alternating turns placing a single piece on any vacant intersection point on the board. If a player successfully forms a horizontal or vertical line of three pieces (a "mill"), they get to instantly remove an opponent's piece from the board, provided that piece is not currently part of another mill itself.
  • Moving Phase: Once all 18 pieces have been played and placed, the mechanics shift. Now, players maneuver their pieces to an adjacent empty point along the drawn lines, trying to continuously build, break, and rebuild mills.
  • Flying Phase: When an unfortunate player gets reduced to exactly three pieces, they unlock the ability to "fly". Their pieces can now jump to ANY vacant spot on the board, ignoring the adjacent lines. To achieve victory, you must dwindle the AI down to just two pieces or completely block all of their moving options!
